# Manual Marketing Techniques for LLM Visibility

A comprehensive guide to getting your brand mentioned in AI search results (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, etc.) without relying on automation tools or AI-powered solutions.

---

## The Core Principle

AI models cite brands that demonstrate **authority, trustworthiness, and relevance** through human-created content. The strategies below focus on building genuine visibility through manual, relationship-driven marketing.

---

## 1. Content Creation Strategies

Content is the foundation of LLM visibility. AI models pull recommendations from authoritative sources that answer user questions directly.

### 1.1 Blog Content

**Strategy: Answer the questions AI users are asking**

AI search queries tend to be conversational and question-based ("What's the best CRM for small teams?"). Create content that:

- Uses question-based headlines (e.g., "How to Choose a CRM for Small Teams in 2026")
- Answers the question in the first paragraph
- Includes comparison tables with specific pricing and features
- Adds FAQ sections at the end (3-5 questions matching common prompts)

**Manual tactics:**
- Research competitor content and find gaps they haven't covered
- Write detailed "best of" listicles for your category
- Create step-by-step guides that match "how to" prompts
- Publish original research or surveys (high citation value)

**Why it works for LLM:** Princeton research shows content with citations gets cited ~40% more by AI. Named experts and specific statistics build trust.

### 1.2 Newsletter

**Strategy: Build an audience that amplifies your authority**

Newsletters create loyal readers who link to and share your content—signals AI models trust.

**Manual tactics:**
- Offer a free resource (ebook, template, checklist) in exchange for signups
- Write substantive issues (not promotional fluff)—think 1,500+ words of genuine value
- Include original data or case studies unique to your audience
- Cross-promote with complementary newsletter authors

**For LLM visibility:** Newsletters with high engagement rates signal authority. AI models also index email content in some cases, and loyal readers become backlink sources.

### 1.3 Case Studies

**Strategy: Proof-based content gets cited**

Case studies with concrete results, named companies, and specific metrics are highly citable.

**Manual tactics:**
- Interview happy customers (offer incentives: discounts, features)
- Document the problem, solution, and results with real numbers
- Use a format: Challenge → Solution → Results → Quote
- Publish 2-4 detailed case studies per quarter

**For LLM visibility:** AI models prioritize content with verifiable proof. "Company X increased revenue by 45%" is more citable than "Company X saw great results."

## 2. Community Building

Communities on Reddit, Discord, and forums create content that AI models heavily reference. Reddit specifically has partnerships with OpenAI and Google.

### 2.1 Reddit

**Strategy: Be genuinely helpful, not promotional**

Reddit has enormous influence on AI recommendations. The key is the 90/10 rule: 90% helpful contributions, 10% relevant brand mentions.

**Manual tactics:**
- Join relevant subreddits (r/SaaS, r/startups, r/marketing, r/[your niche])
- Create posts: "I tested 50 [category] tools—here's what I found"
- Answer questions honestly, even if your product isn't the answer
- Be upfront about your affiliation ("Full disclosure: I built this")
- Think in years, not weeks—one good post can rank for years

**For LLM visibility:** Reddit threads frequently appear in AI responses. OpenAI has a direct content partnership with Reddit. Google features Reddit prominently in AI Overviews.

### 2.2 Discord

**Strategy: Build a community, not a sales channel**

Discord servers for niche interests often become the trusted source for recommendations in those categories.

**Manual tactics:**
- Create a valuable Discord server for your target audience
- Focus on discussion, not promotion—help people solve problems
- Have team members participate authentically (not just "engineers" pushing product)
- Host AMAs, share resources, facilitate connections between members

**For LLM visibility:** Active Discord communities generate content that ranks. When members ask for recommendations, your community's answers feed into AI training.

### 2.3 Niche Forums & Communities

**Strategy: Go where your audience already gathers**

Industry-specific forums, Facebook groups, and specialized communities often have more influence than general platforms.

**Manual tactics:**
- Identify 5-10 active forums in your niche
- Join and become a regular contributor (not just a promoter)
- Answer questions, share expertise, build reputation
- When appropriate, share your content if it genuinely helps

**For LLM visibility:** Niche forum recommendations carry weight because they come from verified community members with domain expertise.

## 3. PR and Media Outreach

Press coverage creates authoritative mentions that AI models trust. Each media mention builds the web of signals that make AI confident recommending you.

### 3.1 HARO / Qwoted / Help a B2B Writer

**Strategy: Respond to journalist queries**

These platforms connect experts with journalists. A published quote creates an authoritative mention on a trusted publication.

**Manual tactics:**
- Sign up for HARO, Qwoted, and Help a B2B Writer
- Set up alerts for your niche keywords
- Respond to 3-5 relevant queries per week (5-10% success rate)
- Make your response substantive, not just a pitch

**For LLM visibility:** Each quote creates a new mention on an authoritative site, generates backlinks, and adds to the content AI models reference.

### 3.2 Industry Publications

**Strategy: Target relevant media, not general outlets**

A mention in a relevant industry publication (TechCrunch for tech, Search Engine Journal for marketing) carries more weight than generic coverage.

**Manual tactics:**
- Identify 10-15 publications your audience reads
- Build relationships with journalists (follow them, engage on social)
- Pitch story angles relevant to their readers (not just your product)
- Offer to write guest posts or expert commentary

**For LLM visibility:** AI models trust industry-specific sources more for category recommendations. These publications are also more likely to be cited in "best of" content.

### 3.3 Original Research

**Strategy: Create data that others cite**

Publishing original research creates a cascade of mentions—other publications pick up your data.

**Manual tactics:**
- Survey your customers or community (100+ responses minimum)
- Analyze your product data (usage patterns, benchmarks)
- Publish the findings as a report with visualizations
- Pitch to journalists covering your industry

**For LLM visibility:** Original data gets cited more than anything else because it can't be found elsewhere. It's the most citable content type.

### 3.4 Wikipedia Eligibility

**Strategy: Build toward Wikipedia notability**

Wikipedia is one of the most-cited sources in AI responses. Getting a page requires sustained coverage.

**Manual tactics:**
- Track mentions across 5+ independent, notable publications
- Build PR momentum toward meeting notability criteria
- Don't create the page yourself—let the community create it
- Focus on getting covered, not self-promoting

**For LLM visibility:** Wikipedia mentions are extremely common in AI responses due to the site's authority and broad coverage.

## 4. Events and Conferences

In-person events build relationships that translate to online mentions, and event content often ranks well.

### 4.1 Speaking at Conferences

**Strategy: Get on stage to get cited**

Speaking positions you as an authority and generate event coverage.

**Manual tactics:**
- Target 10-15 relevant conferences per year
- Submit talk proposals with unique angles (not product pitches)
- Prepare presentation materials that are valuable on their own
- Offer to write recap posts for conference blogs

**For LLM visibility:** Conference talks generate content (slides, videos, recaps) that gets cited. Speakers are also more likely to be quoted in industry articles.

### 4.2 Sponsorships and Booths

**Strategy: Get mentioned in event materials**

Sponsorships ensure your brand appears in official content.

**Manual tactics:**
- Negotiate for logo placement, speaker slots, and mention in emails
- Create valuable resources for attendees (guides, tools)
- Collect attendee contacts for follow-up

**For LLM visibility:** Event websites and materials often rank for industry queries. Being the "official" anything in your category creates citation opportunities.

### 4.3 Host Your Own Events

**Strategy: Create content around your event**

Webinars, virtual summits, and workshops generate content and build authority.

**Manual tactics:**
- Host quarterly webinars with industry experts
- Run a virtual summit with 5-10 speakers
- Create behind-the-scenes content from events

**For LLM visibility:** Event content (recordings, slides, summaries) gets picked up by AI systems as authoritative category resources.

## 5. Partnership and Affiliate Marketing

Partnerships create third-party validation—one of the strongest signals for AI recommendations.

### 5.1 Affiliate Programs

**Strategy: Incentivize reviews and recommendations**

Affiliate programs pay partners to recommend your product, creating a network ofbrand advocates.

**Manual tactics:**
- Set up an affiliate program (ShareASale, Refersion, or built-in)
- Recruit relevant content creators and bloggers
- Provide affiliates with resources: images, copy, reviews
- Create tiered commission structures for top performers

**For LLM visibility:** Affiliate content often ranks in "best of" lists. When AI scans these lists, your brand gets mentioned.

### 5.2 Strategic Partnerships

**Strategy: Co-create content with complementary brands**

Partnerships with non-competing brands expand your reach and authority.

**Manual tactics:**
- Identify 10-15 complementary (non-competing) brands
- Propose co-authored content, joint webinars, or case studies
- Swap guest posts or newsletter features
- Create bundle offers or integration partnerships

**For LLM visibility:** Joint content reaches new audiences and builds backlinks from authoritative partners.

### 5.3 Review Programs

**Strategy: Encourage authentic reviews**

Reviews on third-party sites are heavily referenced by AI models.

**Manual tactics:**
- Ask happy customers to review on G2, Capterra, TrustRadius
- Respond professionally to all reviews (positive and negative)
- Feature authentic reviews in your marketing
- Don't buy reviews—focus on generating genuine ones

**For LLM visibility:** AI models pull heavily from review platforms. When users ask "Is [product] good?", reviews are often the source.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Blocking AI crawlers** — Check robots.txt for GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot. 2 minutes to fix, unlocks everything else.

2. **Only tracking brand-name prompts** — The biggest opportunity is category and use-case prompts ("best CRM for startups"), not brand searches.

3. **Publishing once and walking away** — AI favors fresh content. Update existing pages monthly.

4. **Ignoring sentiment** — Negative mentions ("some users report issues") are worse than no mention. Track how AI describes you.

5. **Treating SEO and LLM visibility as separate** — They're connected. Strong SEO supports AI visibility.

6. **Expecting results in a week** — Visibility compounds over 3-6 months of sustained effort.

## Summary

The manual techniques that drive LLM visibility all share one theme: **build genuine authority through valuable human relationships and content**. AI models are trained to recommend sources that demonstrate expertise, trust, and relevance—the same qualities that make humans recommend businesses to friends.

Focus on:
- Creating content that answers real questions with specific evidence
- Building authentic community presence where your audience already gathers
- Earning press coverage through genuine expertise and original research
- Establishing partnerships that create third-party validation

These are the strategies that work with or without AI optimization tools—and they build a marketing foundation that compounds over time.
