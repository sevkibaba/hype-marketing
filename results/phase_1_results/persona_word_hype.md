# Target Personas for Word Hype

Based on the core features of Word Hype (a dynamic, infinite word game powered by 100% on-device local AI for context-aware hints and vocabulary learning), here are the primary user personas and the specific channels where we can engage them.

---

## Persona 1: The "Active Language Learner" (ELL/ESL)
**The Core Educational User**

*   **Who they are:** Students, young professionals, or ex-pats actively trying to improve their English vocabulary. They are tired of static dictionaries and rote memorization (like Anki or basic Duolingo) and want to learn words through active recall and context.
*   **Why Word Hype appeals to them:** The AI generates context-aware definitions and example sentences, bridging the gap between playing a game and actively studying. Most importantly, the **"My Words"** feature is their killer app: they can save difficult words they encounter in the game and build a personalized, interactive vocabulary list to review later, complete with the specific context in which they originally encountered the word. 
*   **Where to find them:**
    *   **Reddit:** `r/EnglishLearning`, `r/IELTS`, `r/languagelearning`
    *   **TikTok/Instagram/YouTube Shorts:** `#studytok`, `#learnenglish`, `#vocab`. Following accounts that focus on IELTS prep or advanced vocabulary.
    *   **Discord:** Language exchange servers, student study group servers.

## Persona 2: The "Continuous Improvement" Professional
**The Habit Builder**

*   **Who they are:** Ambitious individuals who want to expand their vocabulary to sound more articulate in professional settings, emails, and presentations.
*   **Why Word Hype appeals to them:** They treat Word Hype as a daily brain exercise rather than just a game. The progression from Easy (4-letters) to Hard (6-letters, rare words) offers a tangible sense of improvement. The "My Words" feature allows them to curate a specific list of high-value words to integrate into their daily communication.
*   **Where to find them:**
    *   **LinkedIn:** Engaging with content around communication skills and professional development.
    *   **Newsletters/Blogs:** Readers of productivity and self-improvement newsletters (e.g., James Clear, Morning Brew).
    *   **Reddit:** `r/productivity`, `r/selfimprovement`, `r/VocabWordOfTheDay`.

## Persona 3: The "Edge-AI / Tech Enthusiast"
**The Early Adopter & Evangelist**

*   **Who they are:** Software engineers, AI hobbyists, and tech gadget lovers.
*   **Why Word Hype appeals to them:** The sheer technical novelty of running a 1.1B or 3B parameter LLM (like TinyLlama or Phi-2) *completely locally* on an iOS/Android device offline is fascinating. The ability to generate contextually relevant hints without an internet connection showcases the future of on-device AI.
*   **Where to find them:**
    *   **Platforms:** Product Hunt, Hacker News, Lobste.rs.
    *   **Reddit:** `r/LocalLLaMA`, `r/artificial`, `r/MachineLearning`, `r/reactnative`.
    *   **X (Twitter):** The "build in public" tech community, AI researchers, iOS developers.

## Persona 4: The "Subway Commuter"
**The Utility User**

*   **Who they are:** Anyone with a 30-60 minute underground commute with spotty or zero cell service. 
*   **Why Word Hype appeals to them:** "100% On-Device AI. No Internet Required." is the ultimate value proposition for someone staring at their phone with no signal. They can actively learn and populate their "My Words" list underground, making their commute productive without relying on cloud servers.
*   **Where to find them:**
    *   **Local Subreddits:** City-specific subreddits with big subway systems (`r/nyc`, `r/london`, `r/berlin`) during discussions about commuting.
    *   **App Store Search:** Searching exactly for "offline learning", "offline games", "no wifi vocabulary".

---

### Strategy & Implementation Focus
*Any marketing automations we build should specifically highlight:*
1.  **Learning in Context:** Showing how the AI hints are superior to a static dictionary.
2.  **The "My Words" Feature:** Demonstrating how users can build their own custom vocabulary base to replace flashcards.
3.  **100% Offline Capability:** Emphasizing privacy and utility in low-connection environments.
