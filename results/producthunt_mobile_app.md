# Product Hunt Mobile App Launch Guide

Launching a mobile app on Product Hunt requires a different approach than SaaS or developer tools. This guide covers what works specifically for iOS and Android apps.

---

## 1. How to Launch Any Mobile App on Product Hunt

### The Basic Process

1. **Create your Product Hunt account** (if you don't have one)
2. **Build a "Coming Soon" teaser page** 3-4 weeks before launch
   - Add screenshots, demo video (even a screen recording works)
   - Write compelling copy
   - Set to "Coming Soon" status
   - Collect "notify me" subscribers
3. **Find a hunter** (or self-hunt)
   - Self-hunting is perfectly valid for mobile apps
   - Some makers prefer it for full control

### How to Find a Hunter for Mobile Apps

**Option 1: Self-Hunt (Recommended for Mobile Apps)**
- You're the best hunter for your own app
- You know every feature and can answer questions
- Full control over timing and messaging
- Most mobile app makers self-hunt successfully

**Option 2: Find a Professional Hunter**
- Search PH for hunters with "mobile" or "app" in their profile
- Look for hunters who have launched mobile apps before
- Check their success rate and reviews
- Contact them 2-3 weeks before launch

**Where to Find Hunters:**
- Product Hunt "Hunters" directory
- Twitter/X - search for "Product Hunt hunter"
- PH Discord community
- Maker communities (Indie Hackers, Hacker News)

**How to Approach a Hunter:**
- Be short and professional
- Include your app name and a brief pitch
- Mention any early traction or waitlist numbers
- Ask if they're available for your launch date

**What Hunters Look For:**
- Unique value proposition
- Working product (not just an idea)
- Good visual assets (screenshots, video)
- Your availability for engagement
4. **Prepare your product page** with mobile-specific assets
5. **Launch at 12:01 AM Pacific Time**

### Mobile App-Specific Requirements

| Element | Mobile App Consideration |
|---------|------------------------|
| **Tagline** | Include "iOS" or "Android" if platform-specific |
| **Gallery** | Show app running on actual device, not just mockups |
| **Video** | Screen recording of app in action (under 2 min) |
| **Links** | Direct App Store / Play Store links |
| **Topics** | Add "iOS", "Android", "Mobile App" tags |

### Important Note for Mobile Apps

Product Hunt has a **"Made on Product Hunt" badge** for apps that were built on PH (using their MVP tool). For apps built outside PH, you can still launch—just be clear about your product being a full, shipped app.

## 2. What Makes Mobile Apps Successful on PH

### Key Success Factors

| Factor | Why It Matters |
|--------|---------------|
| **Clear value prop** | Visitors decide in seconds—can they understand what your app does? |
| **Quality screenshots** | Mobile apps live visual. Show the UI, not just mockups |
| **Working product** | iOS/Android reviewers actually download and test apps |
| **App Store links work** | Broken links = lost users |
| **Engaged maker** | Respond to every comment within 30 minutes |

### What Winners Do Differently

- **Launch with 300+ teaser subscribers** (not just 50-100)
- **Dedicate 16+ hours on launch day** to engagement
- **Stagger votes throughout the day** (not front-loaded)
- **Include personal story** in maker comment
- **Prepare for traffic spike** (scale servers if needed)

### Realistic Expectations

| Outcome | Upvotes | Placement |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| Poor | 50-100 | No ranking |
| Average | 200-400 | Top 10 |
| Good | 500-800 | Top 5 |
| Great | 800+ | Product of the Day |

For mobile apps, getting 200-400 upvotes and ranking in Top 10 is a solid result that drives meaningful App Store traffic.

## 3. Mobile App-Specific PH Strategies

### Pre-Launch: Build Your Supporter List

**Who to recruit:**
- Your existing app users (strongest supporters)
- Beta testers
- Social media followers
- Email subscribers
- Other mobile app makers (they understand the struggle)
- Industry connections

**Target:** 200+ minimum, 300+ ideal

### Visual Assets: Mobile-Specific Tips

1. **Device mockups**: Show your app on actual phones (iPhone + Android if both)
2. **GIFs work great**: Show user flows as short GIFs in the gallery
3. **App Store screenshots**: Repurpose high-quality App Store images
4. **Video**: Screen recording showing real app navigation, not a demo

### Make It Download-Friendly

- **Shorten your App Store URL** (use bit.ly or similar)
- **QR code in gallery**: Link directly to App Store/Play Store
- **Consider a web version**: Even a simple landing page helps
- **Offer early access**: "Sign up for beta" converts PH visitors

### Topics to Use

Include these in your submission:
- Mobile App
- iOS (if applicable)
- Android (if applicable)
- Your app's category (Productivity, Health, Finance, etc.)
- AI (if your app uses AI)

## 4. Pre-Launch, Launch Day, and Post-Launch Tactics

### Pre-Launch (30 Days Before)

| Timing | Action |
|--------|--------|
| **Days 30-21** | Create PH teaser page; start community engagement |
| **Days 20-14** | Build supporter list (reach out to users, followers, makers) |
| **Days 13-7** | Finalize assets: screenshots, video, copy |
| **Days 6-3** | Prepare maker comment; test all links |
| **Days 2-1** | Clear calendar; prepare for 16+ hour engagement day |

### Launch Day Timeline (Pacific Time)

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| **12:01 AM** | Launch goes live; post maker comment |
| **12-3 AM** | Personal outreach to top supporters |
| **3-6 AM** | Continue outreach; respond to early comments |
| **6-9 AM** | Social media announcements |
| **9 AM - 12 PM** | Peak period—push engagement |
| **12-3 PM** | Email list blast (staggered) |
| **3-6 PM** | Another push; thank recent voters |
| **6-9 PM** | Final push messaging ("last few hours!") |
| **9 PM - 12 AM** | Close out; thank everyone |

### Do's and Don'ts

**Do:**
- ✅ Thank every voter who leaves feedback
- ✅ Respond to every comment within 30 minutes
- ✅ Share on social media at peak times
- ✅ Stagger outreach throughout the day
- ✅ Make your App Store link prominent

**Don't:**
- ❌ Coordinate obvious voting patterns (PH catches this)
- ❌ Disappear after morning
- ❌ Send mass email all at once
- ❌ Front-load all votes in first hour
- ❌ Use generic/corporate maker comment

### Post-Launch (Days 2-30)

| Timeframe | Actions |
|-----------|---------|
| **Days 2-7** | Follow up with interested users; respond to new comments; create launch retrospective for content |
| **Weeks 2-4** | Update PH page with new features; leverage momentum for press/influencers |
| **Ongoing** | PH pages rank in Google—keep them fresh and accurate |

## Mobile App Launch Checklist

### Before Launch
- [ ] Product Hunt account created
- [ ] Teaser page live with 200+ subscribers
- [ ] App Store / Play Store links tested and working
- [ ] 4-5 gallery images (device mockups, feature screenshots)
- [ ] Demo video (under 2 minutes, screen recording)
- [ ] Tagline under 60 characters with clear value prop
- [ ] Maker comment written (personal story, not corporate)
- [ ] Topics selected (include "Mobile App", platform tags)
- [ ] Team tasks divided for launch day

### Launch Day
- [ ] Launch at 12:01 AM PT
- [ ] 16+ hours dedicated to engagement
- [ ] All comments responded to within 30 minutes
- [ ] Social media active (LinkedIn, Twitter/X)
- [ ] Staggered outreach to supporter list

### After Launch
- [ ] Follow up with leads within 48 hours
- [ ] PH page updated with new features
- [ ] Launch results shared (great for social proof)

---

## Quick Reference

- **Best days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- **Worst days:** Weekends, major holidays, Apple/Google events
- **Launch time:** 12:01 AM Pacific (start of PH's day)
- **Minimum prep time:** 3-4 weeks
- **Minimum supporters:** 200 (300+ recommended)
- **Realistic goal for mobile apps:** Top 10 with 200-400 upvotes
- **Best case:** Top 5 or Product of the Day with 500-800+ upvotes
